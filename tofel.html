TOEFL Course Overview

TOEFL stands for Test of English as a Foreign Language, and as the name suggests, it is an assessment of a candidate’s ability to comprehend, understand and communicate in the English language. In more than 100 countries, TOEFL is taken for studying abroad and other numbers of reasons. TOEFL iBt, the internet-based test, is the most widely taken of all.

The TOEFL test measures all four academic skills — reading, listening, speaking and writing — the way they’re used in the classroom. The language used in the test closely reflects how English is used in everyday academic settings, and the test contains 100% academic content with integrated tasks.

Speaking: Techniques for speaking fluently and coherently, pronunciation practice, and strategies for handling the various speaking tasks in the exam.

Writing: Instruction on how to write essays, summaries, and responses effectively within the time constraints of the exam. This may include grammar and vocabulary building exercises.

Reading: Strategies for quickly comprehending and analyzing written passages, identifying main ideas, supporting details, and understanding the structure of different types of texts.

Listening: Practice exercises to improve listening skills, including note-taking strategies, understanding main ideas, inference, and understanding different accents.

Course Curriculum

Listening

  • Number of question : 3 lectures, some with classroom discussion, each 3–5 minutes, 6 questions each 2 conversations, each 3 minutes, 5 questions each
  • Time : Approximately 36 minutes

Writing

  • Number of question 20-minute integrated task based on what is read and heard 10-minute Writing for an Academic Discussion task to state and support an opinion in an online classroom discussion
  • Time : Approximately 29 minutes

Speaking

  • Number of question :1 independent task to express an opinion on a familiar topic 3 integrated tasks based on what is read and heard Up to 30 seconds to prepare the response, up to 1 minute to respond
  • Time : Approximately 16 minutes

Reading

  • Number of question :2 passages, each 700 words multiple-focus passages (compare/contrast, cause/effect) 10 questions for each passage
  • Time : Approximately 35 minutes
